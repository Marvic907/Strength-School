# Research Evidence Log — Strength School Malta Youth Onboarding

> Scope: high-quality guidance informing intake logic, screening focus, progression notes, and coach decision support.

## 1) International Consensus Statement on Youth Resistance Training
- **Full citation:** Lloyd RS, Faigenbaum AD, Stone MH, et al. Position statement on youth resistance training: the 2014 International Consensus. *British Journal of Sports Medicine*. 2014;48(7):498-505.
- **Link/DOI:** https://bjsm.bmj.com/content/48/7/498 , https://doi.org/10.1136/bjsports-2013-092952
- **Evidence type:** International consensus statement (expert panel + evidence synthesis).
- **Key findings:** Properly supervised youth resistance training is safe and effective; programs should emphasize technical competency, progression, and individualized dosing.
- **Practical implication in app:** Mandatory capture of contraindications, movement competency scores, and maturation/readiness notes before progression planning.

## 2) NSCA Position Statement: Youth Resistance Training
- **Full citation:** Faigenbaum AD, Kraemer WJ, Blimkie CJR, et al. Youth resistance training: updated position statement paper from the National Strength and Conditioning Association. *Journal of Strength and Conditioning Research*. 2009;23(5 Suppl):S60-S79.
- **Link/DOI:** https://journals.lww.com/nsca-jscr/Fulltext/2009/08001/Youth_Resistance_Training__Updated_Position.1.aspx , https://doi.org/10.1519/JSC.0b013e31819df407
- **Evidence type:** Professional position statement.
- **Key findings:** Pre-participation screening, qualified instruction, and appropriate exercise selection are central risk controls.
- **Practical implication in app:** Safety-critical required fields for guardian/emergency data and medical flags; intake cannot pass validation without them.

## 3) IOC Consensus Statement on Youth Athletic Development
- **Full citation:** Bergeron MF, Mountjoy M, Armstrong N, et al. International Olympic Committee consensus statement on youth athletic development. *British Journal of Sports Medicine*. 2015;49(13):843-851.
- **Link/DOI:** https://bjsm.bmj.com/content/49/13/843 , https://doi.org/10.1136/bjsports-2015-094962
- **Evidence type:** IOC consensus statement.
- **Key findings:** Youth programs should be developmentally appropriate, broad-based, and protective against avoidable injury and burnout.
- **Practical implication in app:** Includes goals/sport history and attendance-confidence-readiness notes to support holistic planning, not just physical metrics.

## 4) WHO Physical Activity Guidelines (Children and Adolescents)
- **Full citation:** World Health Organization. *WHO Guidelines on Physical Activity and Sedentary Behaviour*. Geneva: WHO; 2020.
- **Link/DOI:** https://www.who.int/publications/i/item/9789240015128
- **Evidence type:** Global public health guideline.
- **Key findings:** Children and adolescents should engage in regular moderate-to-vigorous activity including vigorous and muscle/bone strengthening activities across the week.
- **Practical implication in app:** Intake fields capture readiness and participation history to support realistic baseline planning aligned to guideline-consistent progression.

## 5) Meta-analysis: Resistance Training in Youth
- **Full citation:** Lesinski M, Herz M, Schmelcher A, Granacher U. Effects of resistance training on physical fitness in healthy children and adolescents: An umbrella review. *Sports Medicine*. 2020;50:1901-1928.
- **Link/DOI:** https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s40279-020-01307-3 , https://doi.org/10.1007/s40279-020-01307-3
- **Evidence type:** Umbrella review/meta-level evidence synthesis.
- **Key findings:** Resistance training generally improves strength and motor outcomes in youth when dosage and supervision are appropriate.
- **Practical implication in app:** Movement competency scoring section (squat/hinge/push/pull/lunge/brace/landing) to inform individualized entry level and technique priority.

## 6) Growth and Maturation Considerations for Youth Development
- **Full citation:** Lloyd RS, Oliver JL. The Youth Physical Development Model: A New Approach to Long-Term Athletic Development. *Strength & Conditioning Journal*. 2012;34(3):61-72.
- **Link/DOI:** https://journals.lww.com/nsca-scj/Fulltext/2012/06000/The_Youth_Physical_Development_Model__A_New.13.aspx , https://doi.org/10.1519/SSC.0b013e31825760ea
- **Evidence type:** Conceptual model with applied synthesis.
- **Key findings:** Development windows differ by maturation stage; training emphasis should be adjusted to biological and psychosocial readiness.
- **Practical implication in app:** Dedicated maturation/readiness notes required in onboarding, plus coach notes for progression rationale.
